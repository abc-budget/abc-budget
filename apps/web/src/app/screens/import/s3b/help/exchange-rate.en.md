The **exchange rate** on a bank statement indicates the relationship between currencies when performing currency
transactions. This happens when a transaction on your account or card was made in a currency other than the account
currency. For example, you made a payment in euros, but your account is opened in hryvnia. In this case, the bank will
convert the amount of the transaction in the account currency at the exchange rate indicated in the statement.
This type of data does not require any additional settings.
