## Binding constraints
- **PM ruling — date operators in UTC:** the prior-art date ops read LOCAL `getDate()/getMonth()`. That
  disagrees with EP-3 `deriveFootprint` (UTC) + the UTC rate key. → evaluate date ops with `getUTC*`,
  CONSISTENT with `deriveFootprint`, so a date-rule and the footprint month never disagree at a boundary.
  **TZ-pin** the operators under hostile `TZ=America/New_York` (the 2.2/EP-3 TZ-pin class).
- **Remove RxJS** — zero RxJS in the ported path; `categorizeRow` stays **SYNCHRONOUS**.
- **NO contract bump** (evaluator is engine-internal). **NO Category persistence** (4.3). **NO override/
  sandbox/UI** (4.4/4.5). Leave clean seams for the override hook (4.4) + typicality post-pass (4.8).

## ⚠️ Scope note (flagged, not blocking)
The prior-art `categorization/service.ts` (in the PORT list) is **persistence orchestration** — it depends
on `ComplexRuleDAO` (create/read/update/batchUpdate), `serializeComplexRule`, the IoC `Container`, and the
RxJS `DecisionTreeService.reload()`. That is inseparable from **Category persistence (4.3)** + the RxJS
path — both role-fenced OUT of 4.2. So 4.2 ports its **pure reorder logic** (`identifyNewOrder` + the
all-have-id / all-found validation over an in-memory order map); the DAO-backed
`createCategorizationRule`/`updateCategorizationRule`/`reorderRules` (DB writes + reload) land in **4.3**.
The 4.2 acceptance criteria (first-match/short-circuit/AND/null, reorder-preserves-IDs, determinism,
no-RxJS, sync, UTC+TZ) are all satisfied without the DAO service. **Flag if you intended the persistence
service in 4.2.**
